hi, nice to meet you ✨

If you don’t already know — I’m Alisha Jain.

I’m a bioengineering student at UC Riverside with a deep interest in how people experience technology — not just how it functions, but how it feels.

My background lives at the intersection of engineering, healthcare, and creative storytelling. I’ve worked on computational modeling projects, biomechanics research, and data-driven tools — but I’m equally drawn to designing calm, human-centered digital spaces that don’t overwhelm the people using them.

I believe the internet doesn’t have to feel loud, cold, or transactional. Thoughtful design, clear structure, and emotional awareness can coexist with technical rigor — and that balance is what I try to bring into everything I build.

what drives my work

I’m especially interested in projects that sit close to real people and real impact — whether that’s modeling physiological systems, building tools that support mental health, or crafting interfaces that feel intuitive and grounding.

My engineering work has trained me to think in systems, constraints, and trade-offs. My creative work reminds me that clarity, pacing, and tone matter just as much as performance.

When those two mindsets come together, the result is work that’s both technically sound and genuinely considerate of the human on the other side of the screen.

outside of work

Outside of engineering and coding, I spend time painting, journaling, curating playlists, and working on narrative game projects rooted in nostalgia and healing.

I’m drawn to warm aesthetics, Studio Ghibli atmospheres, early-2000s internet culture, and design details that feel intentional but unfussy. Those influences quietly shape how I approach both my creative and technical projects.

Thanks for being here — feel free to explore my work, or reach out if something resonates.
